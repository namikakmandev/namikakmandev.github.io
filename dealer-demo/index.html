B · Price realization — money left on the table

Enter units sold and the list / reference price per division (from the DMS and the price list — figures they already have). Realized price = revenue ÷ units. Leakage = (list − realized) × units. Leave list at 0 for divisions where a list price doesn't apply. Read this as vs the dealership's own reference price: for new/used cars, manufacturer campaign support and trade-in over-allowances sit inside these figures, so raw leakage overstates discipline lost — the OEM money that offsets it is quantified in section B2.

B2 · OEM bonus & incentive income — the money the metal margin hides

A franchised dealer's real new-car profit is the manufacturer money paid after the sale: holdback, the volume / registration stair-step bonus (paid retroactively on every unit once a tier is reached), and CSI / margin support. These are brand- and dealer-specific — enter the dealership's own programme. New-car units and revenue are taken live from the trial balance / section B.

C · Variance vs plan — why the gap happened

Enter the budget revenue and budget gross profit per division. The gross gap is split into Volume effect (sold more/less at plan margin) and Rate effect (margin better/worse than plan — usually discounting). Volume + Rate reconciles exactly to the total gross variance.
