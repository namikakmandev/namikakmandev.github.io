4 · OEM bonus & incentive income

What this is: the manufacturer money (holdback, volume stair-step, CSI) that is the real new-car profit the metal margin hides.

A franchised dealer's real new-car profit is the manufacturer money paid after the sale: holdback, the volume / registration stair-step bonus (paid retroactively on every unit once a tier is reached), and CSI / margin support. These are brand- and dealer-specific — enter the dealership's own programme. New-car units and revenue are taken live from the trial balance / price-leakage tab. The tool then reconciles what the programme should pay against the incentive income actually booked in the trial balance — exposing any bonus money left uncollected.

5 · Variance vs plan — why the gap happened

What this is: actual vs the budget you set, split into volume effect (units) vs rate effect (margin / discounting).

Enter the budget (planned) revenue and gross profit per division below. The gross gap vs actual is split into Volume effect (units) — sold more/fewer at plan margin — and Rate effect (pricing) — margin better/worse than plan, mainly discounting (a cost spike can also move it). Volume + Rate reconciles exactly to the total gross variance.
