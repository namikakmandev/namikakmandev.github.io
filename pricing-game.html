The full story & how the simulation works

Your product: a premium 1 kg coffee bag. Unit cost $6.00, shelf price $10.00. You share the market with BrewCo ($10.50, mainstream — it fights back), Velvet Roast ($13.50, premium) — and rumor says a discounter is coming. Last year Atlas made $520k profit; your budget is $572k, phased by month with real seasonality, set before anyone knew what this year would bring.

Each brand gets an attractiveness score from its brand strength minus a price-sensitivity penalty, and market share is split between the brands with a logit (softmax) choice model — with a one-month lag, because customers don't all switch at once. A customer trust index (0–100) scales your demand: sharp hikes hurt it, hikes during a shortage hurt it badly, stable pricing rebuilds it. Strategic moves change trust, brand strength, demand or cash. BrewCo reacts to being undercut; a recession makes everyone more price-sensitive. Profit = (price − unit cost) × volume − $120k fixed costs ± one-off campaign costs. The autopilot benchmark plays the identical year at a frozen $10.00 with the neutral move every month. The 🎯 coach judges your price on a 4-month view, so it never rewards unsustainable gouging.
