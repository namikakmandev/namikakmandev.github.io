Commercial Operations Suite

Ten modules. One data backbone.
Every commercial decision, faster.

A connected suite for commercial and finance teams in pharma-style distribution businesses — from the sales rep's next visit to the CEO's hardest question, built with AI at the core.

10connected modules
3waves — front line to boardroom
1shared data backbone

Most commercial organisations have the same problem: the data exists, but decisions travel slowly. A discount is granted without seeing its net-margin cost. A credit limit is noticed at collection, not at order. A market move is discovered a week late. This suite attacks that lag — each module answers one recurring decision, and all of them share the same backbone, so an answer can travel from region to product to invoice without a single Excel export.

Wave 1 — The front line

Put answers where the customer conversation happens

Quick wins first: field enablement, marketing production and daily operational visibility.

01 Product Knowledge Assistant
An AI assistant that answers product questions only from approved documents — with competitor comparison and objection handling built in. No hallucinated claims, ever.
Answers: "What do I tell this customer, right now, that compliance has already signed off?"
02 Marketing Cockpit
Pick an indication → get an evidence-backed market analysis, then draft presentations, brochures and artwork generated from the same source material.
Answers: "How do we go from insight to campaign material in one sitting?"
03 Operations Tracker
Built here for a hatchery operation — yield, cost, capacity, supplier scorecards, break-even and an owner's report — but the pattern fits any production or logistics unit.
Answers: "Is the operation on plan — and which supplier is dragging it down?"
Wave 2 — Measurable money

Decisions with a currency sign attached

Where the suite pays for itself: pricing, automation, market intelligence and investment decisions — quantified before they're made, not after.

04 Gross-to-Net Simulator
Every discount decision shown as its net-margin impact, live at the negotiation table — designed to run on a live price feed.
Answers: "What does this 5% discount actually cost us?"
05 Process Automation
Reconciliation, track-and-trace reporting and incentive calculations: pulls from different files and systems, computes, and produces the output — 14 minutes, overnight.
Answers: "Why does month-end take a week of copy-paste?"
06 Market Intelligence
Every morning, unprompted: price changes, competitor moves and market spreads — with trends and a map, on management's desk before the first meeting.
Answers: "What moved in the market while we slept?"
07 Investment Decision Engine
Product launch, new facility, or an acquisition — the same engine: IRR and NPV recalculate live, and Monte Carlo simulation tells you the price ceiling.
Answers: "At what price does this deal stop making sense?"
Wave 3 — Strategic depth

Guided selling, prevented losses, and the boardroom layer

Deeper change: every visit planned with intent, bad debt stopped before it ships, and leadership questions answered in one pass.

08 Pre-Call Planner
Shows the rep the opportunity in each account and builds a visit plan tailored to that customer — the tool suggests, the rep decides, and the plan updates through chat.
Answers: "Which customer, which message, which product — before I walk in?"
09 Credit Risk Score
In long-payment-term markets, bad debt is prevented at order entry, not at collection — customer deterioration is flagged up to 60 days early.
Answers: "Should this order ship on these terms?"
10 Executive Q&A
Leadership asks in plain language — the system drills from region to product to invoice and merges multiple companies into a single answer. The layer that sits on top of everything else.
Answers: "Just tell me why the number moved."

Why one backbone matters more than any single module

Each module is useful alone. Connected, they compound: the credit score reads the same customer ledger the gross-to-net simulator prices against; the executive Q&A drills into the numbers the automation produced overnight. That is the difference between ten tools and one system — and it's why the suite is built module by module on a shared foundation, not as ten separate apps.
