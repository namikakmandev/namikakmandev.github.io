You already have tools.
You're still missing answers.

Every company I talk to has an ERP, Excel and usually a BI dashboard. So when someone pitches them "analytics" or "tools", the honest reaction is: we have that already. They're right — and that's not what I sell.

What I sell is a commercial question, answered. Which discounts are burning margin. How much price you must add to survive this year's cost inflation. Which accounts your sales team should call first. Your systems hold the data; what's missing is the pricing and gross-to-net logic that turns it into a decision — and someone who commits to a deliverable and a date.

The gap your systems leave open

Your tools show what happened. They don't answer the question underneath.

Your ERP already shows Revenue and margin by product, customer and month.
What it can't tell you Which 20 customers' discounts and rebates quietly consume the margin the other 200 earn — and what you'd keep if you fixed the worst ten deals.
Your BI dashboard already shows Sales versus budget, updated daily.
What it can't tell you How much price you must add — product by product — to hold margin when your input costs rise 18%, and which products can carry it without losing volume.
Excel can already model Anything, in theory.
What it can't tell you Which model to build, which assumptions survive a management meeting, and who maintains it after the analyst who built it leaves.

That gap — the commercial logic between the data and the decision — is what I bring. The software is just how it's delivered.

What I commit to

Agreed in writing before an engagement starts — so you know exactly what you're buying.

A named decision, not a vague scope

Every engagement is anchored to one concrete question — "which discounts do we cut?", "what price increase holds volume?", "which accounts first?". If we can't name the decision, I don't take the work.

A fixed deliverable on a fixed date

You know on day one what you'll be holding at the end: a decision pack with quantified options and trade-offs — not "areas for further investigation".

Numbers you can defend upstairs

Every figure traces back to your own data, with the working shown. If you want, I present it to your management team with you and take the hard questions.

A tool your team keeps — not a report that dies

The analysis stays behind as a working tool your own people rerun every month with fresh numbers. It runs in the browser: no licences, no servers, no IT project.

Your data stays yours

The tools process everything locally — your sales and margin data never leaves your machines. That's a design principle in everything on this site.

An honest "stop here"

If the diagnostic shows there's nothing worth fixing, I say so and we stop. You lose the cost of a short diagnostic, not a long project.

How an engagement runs

Small first step, clear exit points — you never commit to more than the next stage.

Step 1 · short & fixed-price

Diagnostic

Your data — sales export, trial balance, price lists — through my pricing and gross-to-net lens. Where the margin actually leaks, quantified.

You walk away with: the leakage map and the size of the prize, even if we stop here.

Step 2 · the decision

Decision pack

Quantified options with trade-offs: what to change, what it's worth, what it risks. Presented to your management team, with me in the room.

You walk away with: a decision your team agreed to, backed by numbers from your own data.

Step 3 · it sticks

Tool & handover

The analysis becomes a working tool your team owns — rerun it monthly with new data. I train your people until they don't need me.

You walk away with: a capability, not a dependency.
